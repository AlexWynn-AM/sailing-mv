Day by Day

The float plan*

Four ~4-hour legs. If a town is treating us well, we stay an extra day. If the wind is honking, we hide in a harbor and call it "research."

*"Plan" is a strong word. This is a list of places we'd like the boat to drift near.

1

East Greenwich, RI

Where we load 400 lbs of snacks onto the boat and immediately worry it isn't enough. Greenwich Cove, top of Narragansett Bay.

Cast off · provisioning

Where we start

"Sunwise" lives on a mooring at Greenwich Cove Marina (3 Division St). Dinghy ashore there, or at the town dock / Scalloptown Park — from either it's a short, flat-then-uphill walk into town. This is home base: load up and shove off.

Walk from the dock (all under 10 min)

  • Main Street — one block up the hill: coffee, breakfast, and the last proper grocery/liquor run before the islands. Do your provisioning here.
  • The waterfront restaurants on Water Street — oysters and a drink with the boats in view, right by the landing.
  • Greenwich Cove stroll — flat walk along the marinas to shake out the legs before the first leg.

Skipper's notes: Home mooring is at Greenwich Cove Marina (3 Division St) — that's where "Sunwise" sits. Dinghy ashore to the marina or the town dock. Last real provisioning before the islands; stock up on Main St.

Next leg → East Greenwich to Newport, ~4 hr down the West Passage and round into the East Passage. Mostly protected water — a good warm-up sail.

2

Newport, RI

Sailing capital of America. We will absolutely pretend we belong here. The wharves start at the dinghy dock and never stop.

Mooring or anchor in the harbor

Land the dinghy

Perrotti Park dinghy dock (free, public), right in the main harbor next to Save The Bay's Hamilton Family Aquarium on America's Cup Ave — the Ann Street Pier is the other public dinghy dock just south. Grab a mooring or anchor out and the dinghy ride in is short.

Walk from the dock (all under 15 min)

  • Bowen's & Bannister's Wharf — steps away: dinner, drinks, and the obligatory people-watching. This is the night out.
  • Thames Street — the long shopping/eating spine, right along the water.
  • Washington Square & Brick Market — a few blocks in for a quieter wander and history.
  • The mansions on Bellevue Ave — a ~25-min uphill walk if you're game (The Breakers etc.); otherwise a quick rideshare.

Worth a ride (not walkable): the Cliff Walk and Fort Adams / Castle Hill sunset — grab a car if you have the time.

Skipper's notes: Grab a transient mooring (Oldport Marine VHF 68, Newport Mooring Svc VHF 09) or anchor in the small triangle off Ida Lewis YC (Harbormaster VHF 16). Free dinghy docks for tenders under 14 ft at Perrotti Park & Ann St Pier — or hop the Oldport Harbor Shuttle.

Next leg → Newport to Cuttyhunk, ~4 hr across the open mouth of Rhode Island Sound into Buzzards Bay. The most exposed leg — pick the weather window.

3

Cuttyhunk Island

Population: a rounding error. No cars, no crowds, and oysters that motor out to YOU. Peak smug-sailor energy.

Mooring in the pond

Land the dinghy

Cuttyhunk Town Dock, inside Cuttyhunk Pond. The entire island is walkable — there are basically no cars, so the dinghy is the only commute you'll need.

Walk from the dock (all under 15 min)

  • Up to the lookout — a ten-minute walk to the island's high point for a whole-horizon view over Vineyard Sound. Best at sunset.
  • The general store & seasonal shacks — a handful of tiny spots near the dock for basics and a snack.
  • Church's Beach / the pond bar — short walk to swim and watch the light go.
  • The raw bar boat — you don't even walk for this one: the oyster skiff motors out to your mooring at cocktail hour.

Skipper's notes: Town mooring field (~$65/night for 2026, reserve on Dockwa) or the outer moorings; anchoring in the Pond is tight with marginal, weedy holding. No launch service — your dinghy is the only ride ashore. Harbormaster 508-990-7578.

Next leg → Cuttyhunk to Vineyard Haven, ~4 hr out through Quicks Hole in the Elizabeth Islands and across Vineyard Sound. Time the current in the hole.

4

Martha's Vineyard — Vineyard Haven

The victory lap. We made it — allegedly on purpose. Land in Tisbury; bus and bikes reach the rest.

Harbor mooring / town dock

Land the dinghy

Tisbury Town Dock, right next to the Steamship Authority ferry terminal (1 Water St) — with the Owen Park dinghy dock just up the harbor. Both are steps from Main Street.

Walk from the dock (all under 10 min)

  • Black Dog Tavern & Bakery — right on the harbor beach, a two-minute walk; the obligatory T-shirt is next door.
  • Main Street, Vineyard Haven — shops, the Bunch of Grapes bookstore, food and ice cream.
  • Owen Park — little harbor beach and a bandstand, right at the landing for a swim.

Worth a ride (short bus or bikes): Oak Bluffs for the Gingerbread Cottages and the Flying Horses Carousel (oldest in the US), and Edgartown for the lighthouse and the Chappy ferry. The VTA bus from Vineyard Haven is cheap and frequent; bikes are the fun way.

Skipper's notes: A mooring is almost always available inside or outside the breakwater (Harbormaster VHF 9, launch VHF 72), or anchor off the west breakwater (good holding). Dinghy docks on the north side of the ferry pier and at Owen Park.

Homeward → Reverse the route over a couple of days, or bail out on the ferry and bring the boat back short-handed. Weather, as always, decides.

Actually Useful

Bailing out: getting back to the mainland

So you can decide when to leave (or how to send someone home early without the boat). Newport is easy; Cuttyhunk needs planning; the Vineyard has a ferry that lands 15 minutes from East Greenwich. Confirm every schedule the week of — these shift by season.

East Greenwich

You're home

This is the car lot — where we start and where the cars are waiting. Get back here and you're done; everything below is about reaching this dock from the other stops.

Newport

Easy bail
  • RIPTA Route 14 (West Bay): Newport Transit Center → Providence, roughly hourly, 7 days a week — it runs up the West Bay past T.F. Green and Warwick, i.e. right by East Greenwich.
  • Uber / Lyft: ~30–40 min straight to East Greenwich, always available.
  • Seasonal Providence–Newport ferry if you'd rather be on the water.

Cuttyhunk

Plan ahead
  • Without the boat there's one way off: the Cuttyhunk Ferry to New Bedford (~1 hr, reservations required).
  • Departing Cuttyhunk → New Bedford (summer, Jun 19–Sep 7 2026):
    Mon/Tue 3:00pm · Wed/Thu 11am & 3pm · Fri 11am, 3pm & 7pm · Sat 11am & 3pm · Sun 11:30am & 4pm.
  • So a weekday bail is the 3pm boat; weekends add a late-morning run. Book / confirm: 508-992-0200.
  • From New Bedford it's ~45 min by car/Uber to East Greenwich.

Vineyard Haven

Ferry city
  • Closest to home: the Vineyard Fast Ferry from Oak Bluffs → Quonset Point (North Kingstown, RI), ~95 min, May–Oct — that's ~15 min from East Greenwich. Hop the bus/taxi from Vineyard Haven to Oak Bluffs first (~10 min).
  • Steamship Authority Vineyard Haven → Woods Hole (~45 min, frequent, takes cars), then Peter Pan bus to Providence.
  • Seastreak also runs to New Bedford / Falmouth if timing's better.
