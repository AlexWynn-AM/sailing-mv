Group Chat: "Sailing Summer MV"

Four harbors,
one dinghy,
zero chill.

A weekend(ish) sail from East Greenwich, RI down to Martha's Vineyard, hopping through Newport and Cuttyhunk. Each leg is about a four-hour sail — easy life, with plenty of time to poke around each town. Slow down whenever the weather (or the vibe) says so.

Day by Day

The float plan

Loose by design. Four ~4-hour legs. If a town is treating us well, we stay an extra day. If the wind is honking, we hide.

1

East Greenwich, RI

The dock lines come off here. Greenwich Bay, top of Narragansett Bay.

Cast off · provisioning

The plan

Load the boat, top off water and ice, do the grocery run, and give everyone the dinghy briefing before we even leave the slip. Aim to slip the lines by late morning so the first leg to Newport lands before dinner.

Don't miss

  • Main Street — walk up the hill for coffee and the last real grocery run (this is your best provisioning stop).
  • Greenwich Cove waterfront — oysters and a drink with the boats in view before the long weekend begins.
  • Goddard Memorial State Park beach is a short hop if we want one last shore stroll.

Next leg → East Greenwich to Newport, ~4 hr, straight down the bay. Mostly flat water, good warm-up sail.

2

Newport, RI

The sailing capital. Mansions, mooring fields, and a wharf that does not sleep.

Mooring or anchor in the harbor

The plan

Grab a mooring ball in the harbor (call the launch on VHF), then dinghy in to the wharf. This is the night to go out — everyone else is too.

Don't miss

  • The Cliff Walk — 3.5 miles of ocean on one side, Gilded-Age mansions on the other.
  • The Breakers / Bellevue Ave mansions — absurd in the best way.
  • Bowen's & Bannister's Wharf — dinner, drinks, people-watching.
  • Fort Adams & Castle Hill — sunset with a lighthouse and a lawn.

Next leg → Newport to Cuttyhunk, ~4 hr across the mouth of Rhode Island Sound into Buzzards Bay. Most open-water leg — watch the forecast.

3

Cuttyhunk Island

Population: tiny. The quiet, no-cars, raw-bar-on-a-boat stop.

Mooring in the pond

The plan

This is the decompress stop. Pick up a mooring inside the pond, then do almost nothing on purpose. Watch the sunset from the hill.

Don't miss

  • The raw bar boat — the oyster skiff motors out to your mooring at cocktail hour. Order from your own cockpit.
  • Hike to the high point — ten-minute walk, whole-island views over Vineyard Sound.
  • Sunset + striped bass — Cuttyhunk is legendary for it.
  • The pond & beaches — swim off the stern, walk the bar.

Next leg → Cuttyhunk to Vineyard Haven, ~4 hr through a gap in the Elizabeth Islands (Quicks/Canapitsit) and across Vineyard Sound. Mind the current in the holes.

4

Martha's Vineyard — Vineyard Haven

The big finish. Three towns, gingerbread cottages, the oldest carousel in America.

Harbor mooring / town dock

The plan

Tie up or moor in Vineyard Haven, then spread out. Rent bikes, island-hop the three down-island towns, and find the beach.

Don't miss

  • Oak Bluffs — the candy-colored Gingerbread Cottages and the Flying Horses Carousel (oldest in the US — grab the brass ring).
  • Edgartown — whaling captains' houses, the lighthouse, and the little ferry to Chappaquiddick.
  • Aquinnah (Gay Head) Cliffs — the up-island payoff view.
  • Black Dog Tavern in Vineyard Haven for the obligatory shirt.

Homeward → Reverse the route, or bail out on the ferry and sail the boat back short-handed. Weather decides.
